Common Image Types
- You can only use graphic file formats that browsers support
- JPEG stands for Joint Photographic Experts Group and was established in 1992
- Uses lossless compression to reduce file size Image data is thrown away as they're saved
- GIF stands for Graphics Interchange Format and was introduced in 1987 by CompuServe
- GIFs are 8 bit graphics and are limited to a maximum of 256 colors
- GIFs support transparency and limited animation
- PNG stands for Portable Network Graphics and was developed in 1995 by the open source community over a copyright dispute with GIFs
